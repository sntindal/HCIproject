"How to make egg whites fluffy without a hand-mixer?"

Results for your question:

Sarah V.

I make my eggs fluffy by using a large whisk. Whisk in a circular motion to bring air into the egg whites. Make sure to set the egg whites aside to reach room temperature so they get fluffy faster.

Kayla L.

Oh yeah, I heard the room temperature thing works because the molecules expand or something! How interesting!

Michael S.

I'm not sure what tools you can use besides a hand-mixer. However, I did learn that you can add a pinch of salt or a few drops of vinegar or lemon right before it's about to turn stiff to maintain its shape better.

George T.

Begin whisking by vigorously swishing your whisk back and forth to break up the egg whites until they are foamy. Then start whisking in a more circular motion, lifting the whisk up and out of the egg whites.

Kevin H.

I heard you can use a copper bowl to help the whipped eggs stay whipped up and stiff as well!

Cindy H.

Yes this works well for me! The checmial of the copper bowl reacts with the egg whites to help it whip up and stiffen.

Rachel J.

You can also use two forks if you don't have a whisk. However you will have to use a lot of energy to get the eggs to get fluffy and maintain its stiffness.

Kathy O.

This would be my last resort. I tried the forks once and was not successful.

Gordon R.

Me too! I think the fork just doesn't bring enough air into the egg whites, so it was a lost cause the last time I tried it.
